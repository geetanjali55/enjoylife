An Epic Road Trip Through the American Southwest

This epic journey will wind through four captivating states - Arizona, Utah, New Mexico, and Colorado - hitting both iconic destinations and overlooked gems along the way. Get ready to experience some of the most breathtaking scenery, unique activities, and delicious regional cuisine this part of the country has to offer. Let's explore! Stop 1: Phoenix, Arizona Our road trip kicks off in the Valley of the Sun itself, Phoenix, Arizona. This rapidly-growing desert city makes an ideal first stop to ease into the dry heat while exploring local culture. Must-dos include: - Hiking among the iconic saguaro cacti and beautiful mountain scenery in attractions like Papago Park - Touring the peaceful Heard Museum to learn about Southwest Native American art and history - Grabbing a classic Mexican food fix at spots like The Mission or The Ranch restaurants - Strolling and shopping boutiques/art galleries in downtown's Heritage Square - Staying cool at a scottsdale resort pool for at least one evening
Stop 2: Sedona, Arizona Just a two-hour drive north, you'll be transported to the awe-inspiring red rock paradise of Sedona. This funky little town and its striking natural backdrop have long attracted spiritual seekers, artists, and outdoor adventurers. Key Sedona highlights: - Visiting iconic sites like Bell Rock, Cathedral Rock, and the "Snoopy Rock" formations - Splurging on a pink jeep tour through the incredible Boynton Canyon area - Hiking, mountain biking, or off-roading through the myriad of scenic trails - Wine tasting at local vineyards and checking out upscale galleries along N. State Route 89A - Savoring a decadent breakfast among red rock views at locales like Cress on Oak Creek
Stop 3: Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona No Southwest road trip is complete without gazing over the rim of the Grand Canyon. One of the seven natural wonders of the world, this massive gorge carved by the Colorado River showcases the power and scale of Mother Nature. Things not to miss: - Taking in sunrise or sunset at one of the prime viewing spots along the South Rim - If you have time, hiking partway into the canyon via the Bright Angel or South Kaibab trails - Joining a guided rim walk or mule ride for guided education on the canyon's geology - Catching a bird's eye view on an incredibly scenic helicopter tour - Splurging on an overnight stay at El Tovar or Bright Angel Lodge right on the canyon's edge
Stop 4: Monument Valley Navajo Tribal Park, Utah This famed desert region straddling the Arizona/Utah border was the iconic backdrop for countless Wild West films, and one glimpse of those colossal sandstone buttes explains why. Prepare to have your breath taken away while exploring: - The milky way over towering monoliths like the Mittens on an unforgettable night tour - Vast, otherworldly landscapes on a backcountry jeep or horseback tour led by Navajo guides - Cultural presentations, dances, meals, and artisan demonstrations at "The View" hotel - The incredible Wildcat Trail among mesas and ancient Anasazi ruins
Stop 5: Arches National Park, Utah As we journey into the heart of Utah's red rock paradise, Arches National Park shows off the incredible forces of erosion with over 2,000 kaleidoscopic arches, fins, and pinnacles on display. - Taking postcard-worthy photos at legendary archways like Delicate Arch and Landscape Arch - Hiking the Delicate Arch trail via the easier or more difficult routes depending on fitness level - Exploring paths like Park Avenue for up-close looks at towering rock walls and columns - Squeezing through narrow sections on the Fiery Furnace hike (via guided ranger tour)
Stop 7: Santa Fe, New Mexico Next we head to New Mexico's culture-rich capital of Santa Fe, founded in 1610. Blending Native American, Hispanic, and Anglo influences, this creative city is an absolute delight to explore: - Strolling the historic Plaza area and ducking into art galleries and adobe buildings - Touring Canyon Road's densely packed art galleries and boutiques - Visiting relevation Santuario de Guadalupe, the nation's oldest church - Savoring eclectic Southwestern fare and margaritas at hotspots like The Shed or Cafe Pasqual's - Indulging in the luxurious amenities and pueblo-style ambiance at an iconic resort
Stop 8: Great Sand Dunes National Park and Preserve, Colorado Our last stop will feel worlds away from those initial desert landscapes as we cap off the Southwestern road trip at the tallest sand dunes in North America. - Sledding, sandboarding, or hiking across the undulating 700-foot-tall dunes - Taking in an epic Colorado sunset and nighttime stargazing over the dune field - Hiking the dunes backcountry trails or through nearby alpine forests and meadows - Cooling off with a refreshing dip in Medano Creek's shallow beach area What are you waiting for? Buckle up, fill the tank, and let's hit the road on this unforgettable adventure!
